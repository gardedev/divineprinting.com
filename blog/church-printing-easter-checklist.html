Church Printing Checklist for Easter Sunday Services

📅 March 14, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✝ Divine Printing
← Back to Blog

Easter is the most attended Sunday of the year for most churches. Your printed materials set the tone from the parking lot to the pew. Here's your complete Easter printing checklist.

Exterior — Before They Walk In

Outdoor Easter Banner

A large outdoor banner announces Easter to your entire neighborhood. Place it on your building at least 3 weeks before Easter. ”He Is Risen!” with your service times is a proven, high-impact message. Use gold and white on a dark background for maximum visibility.

Yard Signs

Place directional yard signs along nearby streets in the days leading up to Easter. Include service times and a warm welcome message.

Parking and Directional Signs

If expecting overflow attendance, printed directional signs for parking areas, entrance doors, and overflow seating prevent confusion and make guests feel welcomed.

Interior — The Experience

Stage Backdrop Banner

An Easter stage backdrop transforms your platform into a worship experience. A resurrection scene, cross, or scripture verse creates a powerful visual anchor for your service and photographs beautifully for social media.

Easter Bulletin / Order of Service

A printed order of service helps first-time guests follow along. Include lyrics, scripture references, and a welcome message from your pastor.

Outreach — Before the Sunday

Easter Flyers

Distribute Easter invitation flyers in the weeks prior — in your neighborhood, local businesses, and community centers. A simple ”You're invited to Easter Sunday” with your church name, address, and service time is all you need.

Easter Printing Timeline

  • 6 weeks out: Order outdoor banner and backdrop
  • 4 weeks out: Order yard signs and flyers
  • 2 weeks out: Install outdoor banner, begin flyer distribution
  • 1 week out: Place yard signs, set up lobby banners
  • 3 days out: Print bulletins
